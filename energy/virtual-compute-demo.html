Compute as Virtual Transmission

This interactive demo shows a simple system-level idea: renewable energy is available, but one transmission corridor is congested. First, Grid Enhancing Technologies (GET) relieve that bottleneck. Then the system calculates how much virtual compute can be moved into the region to absorb clean energy without recreating congestion.

What is happening?

Initial state: solar and wind are producing, but one key transmission corridor is congested. That means some renewable energy is at risk of curtailment because the grid cannot move enough power to load centers.

Concept demo only. This is not a power flow model or ISO dispatch model. It is a visual explanation of the coordination logic behind GET + virtual compute.
